FAQ

Here are some of the most asked questions by fellow tourists in Marrakesh

What is the best time to visit Marrakech?
The best time to visit Marrakech is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when the weather is mild and pleasant.
What is the currency used in Marrakech?
The currency used in Marrakech, as well as throughout Morocco, is the Moroccan Dirham (MAD).
Do I need a visa to visit Marrakech?
Visitors from many countries can enter Morocco visa-free for stays of up to 90 days. However, it's always best to check visa requirements based on your nationality before traveling.
What are the must-visit attractions in Marrakech?
Some of the must-visit attractions in Marrakech include Jardin Majorelle, Djemaa el-Fna square, Bahia Palace, Saadian Tombs, and the Koutoubia Mosque.
Is it safe to visit Marrakech?
Marrakech is generally a safe city for tourists. However, as with any destination, it's important to remain vigilant, especially in crowded areas, and to take precautions against petty theft.
What is the traditional Moroccan cuisine like?
Traditional Moroccan cuisine is flavorful and diverse, featuring dishes such as tagine (slow-cooked stews), couscous, pastilla (a savory pie), and various grilled meats. Don't miss trying Moroccan mint tea, a staple beverage.
What should I wear when visiting Marrakech?
Marrakech is a conservative city, so it's best to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites. Women may want to wear loose-fitting clothing that covers the shoulders and knees, while men should avoid wearing shorts in certain areas.
